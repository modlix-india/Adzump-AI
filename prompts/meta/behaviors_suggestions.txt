Your task is to generate behavior targeting seed keywords for Meta Ads based on the business summary below.

These seeds will be used as search queries against Meta's targeting API to surface real behavior segments.

CRITICAL — HOW META BEHAVIOR TARGETING WORKS
Meta behavior segments are a predefined catalogue — they are not keyword-indexed like interests.
Seeds are used as supplementary keyword searches alongside a full catalogue browse.
A seed only has value if it matches terms Meta has already indexed in its behavior segment names.

Meta behavior segments are organised into these fixed categories:
- Purchase behaviour
- Consumer classification (by country and spending tier)
- Digital activities (platform usage, business ownership, tech adoption)
- Travel
- Expats (people who previously lived in a country, now abroad)
- Financial
- Seasonal and events

HOW TO GENERATE GOOD SEEDS
Study the business summary and ask: which behavior categories are relevant to this buyer?
Then generate seeds using Meta's vocabulary patterns — not product descriptions.

Meta's vocabulary pattern for each category:

Purchase and spending (consumer and lifestyle businesses):
Meta uses terms like "engaged shoppers", "high-value goods", "mid and high-value goods",
"consumer classification". Generate seeds that fit this pattern for the business.

Digital activity (B2B, SaaS, tech, small business products):
Meta uses terms like "small business owners", "page admins", "early technology adopters",
"marketing API developers", "new active business". Generate seeds that fit this pattern.

Expats (India-based businesses with diaspora relevance):
Meta uses terms like "lived in India", "expats". Use only if diaspora buyers are relevant.

Travel (only if travel is directly relevant to the buyer):
Meta uses terms like "frequent travellers", "business travellers". Use only if relevant.

IMPORTANT — DO NOT copy the examples above verbatim for every business.
Use them to understand Meta's vocabulary style, then generate seeds appropriate
to this specific business and buyer.

WHAT TO AVOID
- Any term containing a product name (e.g. "real estate buyers", "skincare users",
  "property seekers") — these do not exist as Meta behavior segments
- Invented spending tiers not in Meta's vocabulary (e.g. "premium buyers",
  "luxury spenders") — use "high-value goods" or "engaged shoppers" instead
- Hyper-local or city-level terms
- Device or network types (e.g. "iOS users", "4G users")
- Content engagement terms (e.g. "video viewers", "page likers")

IF BEHAVIOR TARGETING DOES NOT CLEARLY APPLY
If no purchase-intent or spending behavior clearly maps to this business, return an empty seeds array.
Do not generate weak seeds to fill the output.

OUTPUT RULES
- Generate between 5 and 10 seeds
- Each seed must be distinct — no near-duplicates
- Keep seeds concise: 1 to 5 words preferred
- Return strict JSON only, no extra text
